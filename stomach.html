STOMACH PAIN:
A stomach ache is a term often used to refer to cramps or a dull ache in the tummy (abdomen).
It's usually short-lived and is often not serious.
Severe abdominal pain is a greater cause for concern.
If it starts suddenly and unexpectedly, it should be regarded as a medical emergency, especially if the pain is concentrated in a particular area.
If you feel pain in the area around your ribs, read about chest pain for information and advice.
Stomach cramps with bloating are often caused by trapped wind.
This is a very common problem that can be embarrassing, but is easily dealt with.
Your chemist will be able to recommend a product such as buscopan or mebeverine, which can be bought over the counter to treat the problem.
